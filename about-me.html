Welcome to Iza-Ken-Ya!

A blog site about my experiences at different Japanese Restaurants. This isn’t your typical ramen review site, or “The top ten Sushi Restaurants in X”. Here, we will be focusing more on the other hidden treasures within Japanese cuisine.

But first, there are a couple of things I need to get off my chest. First, I am a second-generation Vietnamese American. Other than Vietnamese food, I have an unhealthy obsession with Japanese food. I do not particularly enjoy ramen though, but I do tend to get a bowl every once in a while. I do enjoy sushi, but like I said, I want to focus more on the other dishes that people have heard about.

My love for Japanese food starts is with Izakaya, as you can tell by the name of my blog. Specifically, yakitori. Izakaya is a term used for a restaurant that serves smaller plates in a type of bar setting. You could think of it like Spanish tapas. There is something about this type of Japanese food that speaks to my soul. Not only does the smell of charred meat invoke a euphoric sensation, but the atmosphere that surrounds Izakaya is nothing to be ignored.

To me, it isn’t all about how the food taste, but also the atmosphere. That can range from the decor of the restaurant, to the type of people who eat there. Izakaya is a perfect example of this. Its bar food! Youre surrounded by other people who want to let loose a little, grab a beer after a long day at work, and eat some delicious food that compliments that ice-cold Sapporo. There is nothing better than the moments where you share a meal together with your family, friend, colleges or love one and just simply “shoot the shit”. And I haven’t even gotten started with Udon, Okonomiyaki, Shabu Shabu; to name a few.

I do not want my blogs to be considered a “reviews”. I will not be rating these restaurants, or giving them 3 starts out of 5 because the service was not up to standard. I despise people who give bad reviews to a restaurant for bad service. I just want to know if the food was good! I don’t care if they’re rude to me. As long as they have delicious food, then all is forgiven. I want my blogs to be more about how I enjoyed the food, the restaurant, and just my general opinion on the place. But remember that this is “my” own opinion. Im here to encourage people to make their own opinion. To have you try something other than ramen and sushi. If my blog gives someone the courage to step outside their comfort zone and order that chicken knee cartilage, then my job is done.

I hope you enjoy what I write. This is my first blog, ever, so please bear with me while I grow.

With Love,

Ken
